Digital Media Store Sales Analysis

Project Date

Oct 15, 2019

Category

SQL, Data Visualization, Python, Analytics

nbviewer View Code

Background

In order to make informed decisions, businesses often rely on actionable data-driven insights. In this project, I analyze a digital media store's relational database to answer the following questions:

  • Which albums should be purchased in the future to retain or improve sales?
  • Which countries have the most potential for growth in sales?
  • Do more customers purchase whole albums, or collections of one or more individual tracks?

The database was queried using SQL (SQLite) and then visualized using Python for interpretability.

Conclusions

Results of the analysis demonstrated the following:

  • Maintaining a high inventory of rock albums should be prioritized since more than 50% of individual sales are of this genre.
  • Marketing efforts outside of the USA should be focused on the Czech Republic, India, and the UK since these customers generally produce higher sales relative to the average client, as well as low demographic representation.
  • 81% of customer transactions consist of a collection of individual track purchases, rather than whole album purchases.

Full details of the analysis are presented in the notebooks linked at the top of the page.
